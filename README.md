# THE PHASMONIC CONVERSION

THE PHASMONIC CONVERSION ("PHAS" as in "has","MONIC" as in "harmonic") is a set of house rules for the fifth edition of the world's greatest superhero roleplaying game.

# Characteristics

Characters have five primary characteristics and two figured characteristics. These characteristics may each be increased at a fixed cost.

- **Strength** 10, 1 point per +1
- **Dexterity** 10, 3 points per +1
- **Toughness** 10, 3 points per +1
- **Mind** 10, 2 points per +1
- **Presence** 10, 1 point per +1
- -
- **Stamina** (Toughness x2), 1 point per +1
- **Defense** (Toughness / 5), 2 points per+1

## Changes

The fourteen standard characteristics have been reduced to seven.

- **Speed**, **Recovery**, and **Endurance** have been removed.
- -
- **Constitution** and **Body** are combined into **Toughness** (TGH).
- **Intelligence** and **Ego** are combined into **Mind** (MND).
- **Presence** and **Comeliness** are combined into **Presence** (PRE)
- **Body** and **Stun** are combined into **Stamina** (STA).
- **Physical Defense** and **Energy Defense** are combined into **Defense** (DEF).
- -
- **Strength** (STR) and **Dexterity** (DEX) are unchanged.

# Combat

## Order of Play

Each Segment:

- The players decide who among them goes first.
- Each player uses their Phase.
- The GM uses the Phases of the NPCs
- Repeat.

Each Segment is roughly two seconds, with six Segments composing a Turn, five Turns making a minute, and so on.

## Damage

When rolling normal damage, the dots on the dice are counted as Stamina damage.

The character's Defense is subtracted from the damage, and the remaining damage is subtracted from the character's Stamina.

## Recovery

As a Full Phase action, a character may make a Toughness Roll to attempt a Recovery. Success restores 1 Stamina and stabilizes their condition. Failure has no effect.

An unconscious character makes a roll for Recovery at the end of each Turn.

## Unconsciousness

A character reduced to 0 Stamina or less by a normal attack is unconscious.

At the end of each Turn, an unconscious character automatically makes a roll for Recovery. If this restores their Stamina above 0, they regain consciousness and may act normally on their next Phase.

An adjacent ally may spend a Half Phase attempting to revive an unconscious character. This grants the unconscious character a +1 modifier to their next roll for Recovery.

## Death and Dying

A character reduced to 0 Stamina or less by a Killing Attack is unconscious and immediately makes a Toughness Roll. Failure means the character is dying. Success means the character is stable.

A dying character loses 1 Stamina at the end of each Turn and then makes a roll for Recovery. Once stabilized, the character follows the normal rules for unconsciousness.

An adjacent ally may spend a Half Phase stabilizing a dying character. No roll is required.

A character dies when their Stamina is reduced to a negative value equal to their maximum Stamina.

# Powers

## Defense Powers

As a result of combining PD and ED (and resistant PD and resistant ED) into Defense, Powers which grant Defense cost twice as much per point.

- **Armor** costs 3 points for +1 Defense
- **Damage Resistance** has been removed
- **Force Field** costs 2 points per +1 Defense
- **Force Wall** costs 5 points per +4 Defense, +2 Flash Defense, +2 Mental Defense, or +2 Power Defense
